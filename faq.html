Quick answers

Common questions about Tennessee remote notarization

What is an online notary public in Tennessee?

The Tennessee Secretary of State explains that online notarization is a notarial act performed by means of two-way video and audio conference technology. In practical terms, that means the notary and signer are not in the same physical place, but still interact live through approved technology.

Official Tennessee Secretary of State FAQ

What is the difference between an e-notary and a remote online notary?

Tennessee distinguishes between the two. In e-notarization, digital signatures are used, but the signer still appears in the physical presence of the notary. In remote notarization, the signer is not physically present and instead appears through audio and video technology.

Official Tennessee Secretary of State FAQ

What fee can be charged for an online notarization in Tennessee?

Tennessee Secretary of State guidance states that a fee can be required not to exceed $25 for each online notarization under Tennessee Rule 1360-07-03-.03. For customers, the important distinction is that statutory notarial fees and broader service fees are not always the same thing, so the final structure should be disclosed clearly before the appointment.

Official Tennessee Secretary of State FAQ

How do you become an online notary public in Tennessee?

Tennessee says you must already be an active notary public, review the applicable statutes and rules, contract with an appropriate third-party vendor before applying, and then apply for an online notary commission through the Tennessee Secretary of State.

Official Tennessee Secretary of State guide

Does Tennessee publish an approved vendor list for remote online notaries?

No. The Tennessee Secretary of State says it does not recommend or endorse any particular third-party vendors. Whatever platform is used should support identity proofing, credential analysis, electronic seals and signatures, and required recording storage.

Official Tennessee Secretary of State FAQ

Does Tennessee offer e-notaries?

Tennessee Secretary of State guidance says the state offers notaries and remote online notaries. That matters because people often use “e-notary” and “online notary” interchangeably when Tennessee treats them differently.
