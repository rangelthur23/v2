Official Tennessee guidance

What Tennessee says about online notary fees

The Tennessee Secretary of State states that a fee can be required not to exceed $25 for each online notarization under Tennessee Rule 1360-07-03-.03. The same office explains that online notarization in Tennessee is a notarial act performed by means of two-way video and audio conference technology, meaning the notary and signer are not physically in the same place during the act.

For official state references, see the Tennessee Secretary of State online notary fee FAQ and the online notary public FAQ.

Where confusion happens

Why some notary pricing looks higher than the state fee

Searchers often assume the Tennessee fee guidance and the total amount shown on a service website are the same thing. In practice, providers may separately disclose broader service pricing tied to travel, appointment structure, premium location handling, mileage, scheduling, or other operational costs.

The right way to evaluate a provider is to check whether the provider clearly shows what is included, which optional charges may apply, and whether the estimate is visible before you submit your request.
